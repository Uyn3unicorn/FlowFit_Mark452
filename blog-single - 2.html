Period-friendly workouts for every phase

10 March 2025 Maria Smith Tag: Body Fitness, Menstruation Care

      Feeling super energetic one week, then completely drained the next ? Instead of pushing through exhaustion, start understanding your body and syncing workouts with your cycle ! Training in alignment with your hormonal fluctuations helps you optimize your energy, improve performance, and reduce injury risks. Stop fighting your body: work with it to get the best fitness results ! 👍

1️⃣ Week of menstruation → your body has a lot to do and is tired: take care of it and allow it to recover with light, low-impact activities 😎

  • Ease cramps, improve circulation, and support recovery: Light yoga, stretching, and walking 🧘‍♀️
  • If you're one of the lucky ones who still feel energized: Opt for moderate strength training (while always listening to your body and avoiding overexertion) 📶
  • Rest days are 100% okay: Take them if you feel the need 💤

2️⃣ Follicular phase → estrogen levels are rising, energy and endurance are back! 🔋

  • Your metabolism is efficient, your body recovers faster: Best time to push yourself with workouts combining strength and speed 🏋🏼‍♀️
  • Optimal workouts: Heavy lifting, HIIT, and cardio like running or cycling 🫀
  • Take advantage of this energy boost: Practice dance, pilates, and other fun, high-energy workouts! 💃

3️⃣ Ovulation (≈ days 15-17 of the cycle) → peak performance. This stage brings the best energy and power output: strength, endurance, and coordination are at their highest! 📈

  • Testosterone levels spike: Best time for max strength training combining PR lifts and explosive workouts 💣
  • High-intensity cardio: Sprints, bootcamp, or CrossFit, depending on your personal preferences! ⚡️

4️⃣ Luteal phase → here, it's important to preserve and take care of your body as it undergoes a drop in energy and PMS symptoms 🧖‍♀️

  • Moderate strength training: Adjust workouts with lighter weights and more reps 🙌
  • Less intense activities to reduce stress and bloating: Yoga and pilates 🙆‍♀️
  • Other low-impact activities to stay active without overloading your system: Walking, swimming, or cycling 🏊🏼‍♀️
