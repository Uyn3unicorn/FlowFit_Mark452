1. Track Your Cycle

Understanding your cycle is the key to improving and maximizing your workouts. Use an app or a journal to monitor your phases:

  • Menstrual phase (Days 1-6): Low energy, ideal for light movement like yoga or walking.
  • Follicular phase (Days 7-12): Peak energy, great for long runs and intensive training.
  • Ovulation phase (Days 13-15): High energy and endurance levels, also good for high-intensity activities.
  • Luteal phase (Days 16-28): Energy dips, so focus on moderate exercises, such as pilates, outdoor walks/hikes, and strength training (low to moderate weight and higher reps).
2. Monitor Nutrition and Hydration

What you eat and drink throughout your cycle can affect your energy levels, recovery, and overall performance. Focusing on the right nutrients and staying hydrated will give you the boost that you need.

  • Increase iron-rich foods: Spinach, red meat during menstruation.
  • Incorporate magnesium-rich foods: Dark chocolate and almonds to ease PMS symptoms.
  • Stay hydrated: Reduce bloating and cramps.
  • Boost protein intake: In the follicular phase to support muscle growth.
  • Focus on healthy fats: Avocados, nuts in the luteal phase to stabilize energy.
3. Listen to Your Body

Your body goes through natural changes. Pay attention to signals like fatigue, soreness, and mood changes and adjust intensity and rest accordingly.

  • Modify workouts based on energy levels: Push harder when you feel strong and ease up when needed.
  • Recognize the signs of overtraining: Multiple signs, such as persistent fatigue and trouble sleeping, may indicate that you need some rest.
  • Give importance to your recovery: Stretching and gentle movement can help reduce discomfort and improve flexibility.
  • Be easy on yourself: Some days will be harder than others. Listening to your body without guilt will lead to better long-term fitness results.
